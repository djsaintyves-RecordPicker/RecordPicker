Random picker

Random vinyl record picker app

Random choice becomes useful when it understands a little about your collection.

App StoreView screenshots

A purely random draw can be fun, but it quickly becomes frustrating if the app suggests an unavailable record, the same artist as yesterday, or an album that does not fit the moment at all.

A good picker needs to mix chance with simple rules. Record Picker keeps the pleasure of surprise while respecting exclusions, favorites, filters, and the real state of the collection.

Filtered randomness

The draw can take years, genres, styles, favorites, availability, and temporary exclusions into account. You do not choose the record directly, but you choose the playing field.

That is especially useful for larger collections: chance brings forgotten albums back without leaving the listening context completely.

Favorites, exclusions, and artist variety

Record Picker can favor favorites, limit the draw to favorites, or avoid repeating the same artist. Temporary exclusions also let you set aside a record or criterion without permanently changing the collection.

The result is not just random. It is closer to the way you actually listen.

On iPhone, iPad, and Apple Watch

The draw is available on iPhone and iPad, with wider views for browsing the record bin and filters. The Apple Watch companion keeps essentials on the wrist.

The goal is simple: choose the next record quickly without turning listening into database work.

Key takeaways

  • Animated draw from the available collection.
  • Filters by year, genre, style, and favorites.
  • Temporary exclusion of records or artists.
  • Draw history to avoid invisible habits.
