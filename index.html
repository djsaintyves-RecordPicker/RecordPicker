Privacy Policy - RecordPicker v 1.1.1

Last updated: June 17, 2026

RecordPicker is designed to keep your music collection private. Your record, CD, and album collection is stored locally on your device.

Collection data

The albums you add, import, edit, favorite, exclude, or draw are stored on your device. This may include album titles, artist names, release information, genres, styles, tags, notes, ratings, listening history, cover artwork, and related collection metadata.

RecordPicker does not operate a user account system and does not upload your collection to a RecordPicker server.

Camera and barcode scanning

RecordPicker may use the camera to scan barcodes when you choose to add an album this way. Camera images are not stored or shared by RecordPicker.

Metadata and cover searches

When you start a metadata or cover search, RecordPicker may contact external music metadata services such as MusicBrainz, Cover Art Archive, or Discogs to search for album information and artwork.

These requests may include the search terms or barcode you provide, and the external service may receive technical information normally sent with network requests, such as your IP address. RecordPicker uses these services only when you initiate a lookup or artwork search.

On-device suggestions

RecordPicker may use Apple on-device models, when available, to help suggest albums by mood. This processing happens locally on your device. When on-device models are not available, RecordPicker calculates suggestions locally from your collection metadata, favorites, tags, genres, styles, and recent draws.

Apple Watch companion

If you use the Apple Watch companion app, RecordPicker may sync essential collection and draw information between your iPhone and Apple Watch using Apple's device communication features. This information remains part of your local app experience and is not sent to a RecordPicker server.

Backups, imports, and exports

RecordPicker may let you import, export, back up, or restore collection files. These files are created, selected, and managed by you. If you store or share them using iCloud Drive, Files, AirDrop, email, or another service, that service's privacy practices apply.

Tracking, advertising, and analytics

RecordPicker does not use advertising tracking. RecordPicker does not include third-party advertising SDKs.
