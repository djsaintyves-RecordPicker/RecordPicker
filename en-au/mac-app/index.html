Record Picker for Mac

The calm, native way to catalog your vinyl and decide what to spin tonight.

Get the iPhone and iPad appExplore current features

Record Picker brings your record collection to the Mac with a fast catalog, a mood-driven recommendation engine, and automatic critical-review enrichment, all in a clean, native SwiftUI app.

No ads, no tracking, no subscription. Your library syncs privately across Mac, iPhone, and iPad through iCloud.

Catalog, beautifully

Browse your collection as a zoomable cover mosaic or a sortable list. Edit any field inline: title, artist, genres, tags, year, format, label, country, notes, and custom covers.

Find anything, instantly

Full-text search includes a live results navigator, highlighted matches, and step-by-step browsing. Eight one-click sections are wired to Command-1 through Command-8.

Pick a record by mood

Type a moment, such as rainy Sunday afternoon, electro night, or Britpop. Mood Pick recommends the right album from your shelves and avoids repetition.

Highlights

  • Automatic critical reviews. Fill in critical notes from Apple Music Editor's Notes, Last.fm, Wikipedia, and Discogs. Choose the source order, combine sources, and protect hand-written reviews.
  • Keep your library clean. A Data Quality panel flags duplicates, missing covers, years, formats, genres, reviews, and more, with one-click and bulk fixes.
  • Listen anywhere. Open any album on Apple Music, Spotify, Deezer, Tidal, Qobuz, YouTube Music, Amazon Music, or SoundCloud straight from the record card.
  • Statistics and insight. See top artists, labels, years, and formats, with optional narrative analysis powered by Apple Intelligence.
  • Private by design. SwiftData and CloudKit keep your collection, custom covers, and history in sync across your devices with no account and no third-party server.
  • Import and backup. Import from CSV, including Discogs-compatible files, then back up and restore as JSON. Record Picker is localized in 25+ languages.

Requirements

  • macOS on Apple silicon and Intel.
  • Apple Music review enrichment requires an Apple Music subscription and authorization.
  • Last.fm enrichment uses a free personal API key.
